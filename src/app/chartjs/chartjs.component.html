Note

  • Free, MIT Licensed, Open Source: Chart.js is completely free to use and is distributed under the MIT license, making it an excellent choice for both commercial and non-commercial projects. Its open-source nature also ensures a large community and continuous improvements.
  • Supports Variety of Interaction Options, Including Events: Chart.js provides a variety of built-in interaction options, such as tooltips, hover effects, and click events. Developers can easily hook into these interactions to create dynamic and responsive charts.
  • Accessibility Needs Manual Enhancement: While Chart.js is a powerful tool, developers need to manually add ARIA attributes and other accessibility features to ensure that charts are accessible to all users, including those using screen readers.
  • Moderately Flexible Customization: Chart.js allows for a moderate level of customization. It offers control over axes and supports callbacks for every lifecycle stage of an axis, providing developers with the flexibility to adjust how data is presented.
  • Basic Styling Support: The library supports basic styling options, such as colors and borders. However, more advanced styling might require custom implementations or additional tools to achieve complex visual effects.
